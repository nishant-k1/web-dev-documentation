# I ask one system design question in every frontend interview.

Not "design Twitter" or "design a URL shortener."

Something smaller. Something they can finish in20utes.

"Design the frontend for a product listing page. Assume the API gives you a list of items. How do you structure components, state, and data flow? Walk me through your thinking."

What I'm listening for:

→ Separation of concerns: Do they split UI, data fetching, and business logic?
→ Edge cases: Loading, errors, empty states. Do they mention them without being prompted?
→ Scalability: "What if we add filters, sorting, infinite scroll?" How do they extend the design?
→ Trade-offs: Do they say "we could do X, but Y is better because..."?

The answer doesn't have to be perfect. I care more about how they think than the exact diagram.
